# Using Rushd — Module 1 Self-Assessment

## What this is

This is a guided self-reflection conversation about how you approach
relationships and what you're looking for in a partner. It's **not**
therapy, **not** a clinical assessment, and **not** a fatwa — just a
structured way to think out loud with the help of an AI, and end up with
something written down that you can reflect on.

It takes about **20–40 minutes**. Your answers stay private on the host's
own machine — nothing is shared with anyone else automatically.

## Getting started

You'll receive a personal invite link — it only works for you. To begin:

1. Click your invite link.
2. Choose your language (Arabic or English).
3. Enter your age and gender.
4. Start the conversation.

## The conversation

An AI will ask you questions about your past experiences, your emotional
patterns, and what you look for in a partner.

- Answer honestly — there are no right or wrong answers.
- The conversation adapts to what you share; there's no fixed script it's
  working through.
- As you go, you'll see which themes have been explored so far.
- Type `/done` whenever you feel ready to wrap up — or the AI will guide
  the conversation toward a natural stopping point on its own.

## Your result page

Once the conversation ends, your profile is generated automatically. It
includes:

- A self-awareness summary
- Your strengths and weaknesses
- What to look for in a partner
- Ratings grouped into three sections:
  - **Emotional-hunger risk** — shown first, most prominent
  - **Maturity levels** — 5 dimensions
  - **Needs** — 8 dimensions

Each rating comes with a one-line description of what that dimension
means, and a short justification (1–2 sentences) grounded in what you
actually said during the conversation — not a generic explanation.

You can also view the full conversation history from the result page if
you want to look back at what you said.

## Revisiting your link

If you click your invite link again — on any device, any time — you'll go
straight to your completed profile. You won't be asked to start the
conversation over.

## Important limits

- This is a self-reflection aid, not a diagnostic tool.
- It cannot tell you whether a specific person is right for you.
- If anything in the conversation touches on something difficult, you're
  free to stop at any point — there's no obligation to finish in one
  sitting or at all.
